## Background

Hepatocellular carcinoma ("HCC") is the third leading cause of cancer-related mortality worldwide. First-line systemic therapy with tyrosine kinase inhibitors produces median overall survival of approximately 13 months. Selumetinib-XB, a novel allosteric MEK1/MEK2 inhibitor, has demonstrated preclinical activity against RAS-mutated HCC cell lines and acceptable safety in a Phase I dose-escalation study.

## Methods

We conducted a multicenter, double-blind, placebo-controlled Phase III trial across 48 academic cancer centers in North America, Europe, and East Asia. Adult patients (age ≥ 18) with histologically confirmed Child-Pugh A HCC, Barcelona Clinic Liver Cancer stage B or C, and no prior systemic therapy were randomized 1:1 to receive either **selumetinib-XB 75 mg orally twice daily** or matching placebo, in addition to standard-of-care sorafenib. The primary endpoint was **overall survival**. Secondary endpoints included **progression-free survival**, **objective response rate** per RECIST v1.1, and **time to symptomatic progression**. Safety was assessed according to CTCAE v5.0.

## Results

Between March 2022 and November 2023, 612 patients were enrolled (306 per arm). Baseline characteristics were balanced. Median overall survival was **17.4 months** (95% CI 15.2–19.8) in the selumetinib-XB arm versus **12.9 months** (95% CI 11.1–14.6) in the placebo arm (hazard ratio 0.68; 95% CI 0.55–0.84; p < 0.001). Median progression-free survival was 7.2 months versus 4.1 months (HR 0.61; p < 0.001). Objective response rate was 28.4% versus 11.7%. Grade ≥ 3 adverse events occurred in 51.3% of selumetinib-XB-treated patients and 38.4% of placebo-treated patients; the most common were hypertension (14.2%), hand-foot syndrome (9.8%), and elevated transaminases (7.6%).

## Conclusions

Selumetinib-XB in combination with sorafenib significantly prolonged overall survival compared with sorafenib alone in previously untreated patients with advanced hepatocellular carcinoma. The safety profile was consistent with the known class effects of MEK inhibitors and with sorafenib monotherapy.
