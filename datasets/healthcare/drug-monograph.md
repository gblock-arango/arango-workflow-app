# Drug Monograph — Lisinovar (lisinopril-valsartan, fixed-dose combination) [Synthetic]

> Synthetic drug monograph authored for AOE testing. "Lisinovar" is fictional; the underlying active ingredients are real but dosing, indications, and contraindications in this document are not intended for clinical use.

## Indication

Lisinovar is indicated for the treatment of **essential hypertension** in adult patients whose blood pressure is not adequately controlled on monotherapy with an angiotensin-converting enzyme ("ACE") inhibitor or an angiotensin II receptor blocker ("ARB").

## Dosage and Administration

The recommended starting dose is **lisinopril 10 mg / valsartan 80 mg once daily**, administered orally with or without food. The dose may be titrated after 4 weeks to a maximum of lisinopril 40 mg / valsartan 160 mg once daily based on blood pressure response. Dose adjustment is required in patients with moderate renal impairment (creatinine clearance 30–60 mL/min); the drug should not be used when creatinine clearance is below 30 mL/min.

## Contraindications

- Known hypersensitivity to ACE inhibitors, ARBs, or any excipient.
- History of angioedema.
- Pregnancy. Drugs acting on the renin-angiotensin system may cause fetal harm when administered during the second and third trimesters.
- Concomitant use with aliskiren in patients with diabetes mellitus.

## Warnings and Precautions

**Hyperkalemia.** Serum potassium should be monitored at baseline, at 4 weeks, and periodically thereafter. The risk of hyperkalemia is increased in patients with renal impairment, diabetes, or concomitant potassium-sparing diuretics.

**Renal function.** Acute changes in serum creatinine may occur, particularly in patients with renal artery stenosis, severe congestive heart failure, or volume depletion.

**Hypotension.** Symptomatic hypotension may occur after the initial dose in volume-depleted patients; treatment should be initiated under medical supervision.

## Drug Interactions

Concomitant use with potassium supplements, potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone, amiloride), or salt substitutes containing potassium may lead to clinically significant hyperkalemia. Concomitant use with non-steroidal anti-inflammatory drugs ("NSAIDs"), including selective COX-2 inhibitors, may reduce the antihypertensive effect and increase the risk of renal dysfunction. Lithium clearance may be reduced when Lisinovar is co-administered; serum lithium levels should be monitored.

## Pharmacokinetics

Lisinopril is not metabolized and is excreted unchanged in the urine. Peak plasma concentrations are achieved approximately 7 hours after oral administration. Valsartan undergoes minimal hepatic metabolism; approximately 83% of the absorbed dose is excreted unchanged in the feces and 13% in the urine. The elimination half-life of valsartan is approximately 6 hours.

## Storage

Store at 20–25 °C (68–77 °F) in the original blister pack. Protect from moisture. Keep out of reach of children.
