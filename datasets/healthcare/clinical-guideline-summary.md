# Clinical Guideline — Management of Atrial Fibrillation in Adults (Synthetic Summary)

> Synthetic clinical-guideline summary authored for AOE testing. The issuing society and recommendation classes below are fictional.

**Issuing society:** International Society for Cardiovascular Practice ("ISCP")
**Guideline version:** 3.1 (2025)
**Scope:** Adult patients (age ≥ 18) with diagnosed atrial fibrillation ("AF"), excluding post-operative AF within 30 days of cardiac surgery.

## 1. Diagnosis

| Recommendation | Class |
| --- | --- |
| Confirm AF with a 12-lead ECG; single-lead rhythm strips may suggest but not confirm AF. | I |
| In patients with cryptogenic stroke, use ambulatory cardiac monitoring for at least 14 days to screen for paroxysmal AF. | IIa |
| Screen for AF in patients ≥ 65 years at routine clinic visits by pulse palpation. | IIa |

## 2. Risk Stratification

### 2.1 Stroke Risk

The **CHA₂DS₂-VASc** score should be used to estimate annual stroke risk in non-valvular AF. Oral anticoagulation is recommended when the CHA₂DS₂-VASc score is ≥ 2 in men or ≥ 3 in women (Class I). Anticoagulation may be considered at a score of 1 in men and 2 in women after shared decision-making (Class IIb).

### 2.2 Bleeding Risk

The **HAS-BLED** score should be computed at each follow-up visit. A score ≥ 3 identifies patients in whom modifiable bleeding risk factors (uncontrolled hypertension, labile INR, concomitant antiplatelet therapy, excessive alcohol use) should be addressed, but does not on its own justify withholding anticoagulation.

## 3. Stroke Prevention

| Recommendation | Class |
| --- | --- |
| Direct oral anticoagulants ("DOACs") — apixaban, dabigatran, edoxaban, rivaroxaban — are preferred over warfarin in non-valvular AF. | I |
| Warfarin is preferred in patients with moderate-to-severe mitral stenosis or a mechanical heart valve. | I |
| Aspirin monotherapy is not recommended for stroke prevention in AF. | III (Harm) |

## 4. Rate and Rhythm Control

### 4.1 Rate Control

Resting heart rate below **110 beats per minute** is acceptable in asymptomatic patients. Beta-blockers and non-dihydropyridine calcium channel blockers are first-line agents. Digoxin may be added when rate control is inadequate.

### 4.2 Rhythm Control

Catheter ablation is recommended for symptomatic paroxysmal AF refractory to or intolerant of at least one Class I or III antiarrhythmic agent (Class I). Catheter ablation may be considered as first-line rhythm control in selected patients with symptomatic paroxysmal AF (Class IIa).

## 5. Comorbid Management

Modifiable risk factors contribute substantially to AF burden. The guideline recommends structured programs addressing **obesity** (target BMI < 27 kg/m²), **obstructive sleep apnea** (screen with STOP-BANG; treat moderate-to-severe OSA with CPAP), **hypertension** (target office blood pressure < 130/80 mmHg), and **alcohol consumption** (≤ 3 standard drinks per week).
