**Issuing authority:** Federated Customs and Trade Administration ("FCTA")
**Bulletin number:** CB-2026-11
**Effective date:** June 1, 2026
**Supersedes:** Customs Bulletin CB-2022-07

## 1. Background

The FCTA has reviewed the classification of lithium-ion battery modules intended for use in electric passenger vehicles in light of recent trade remedy investigations and technological developments in module-level thermal management. This bulletin sets forth the updated binding tariff ruling applicable to such goods upon entry into the customs territory.

## 2. Scope

This bulletin applies to finished lithium-ion battery modules that meet **all** of the following criteria:

1. Rated nominal voltage between 3.5 V and 4.2 V per cell.
2. Assembled from pouch, prismatic, or cylindrical cells into a single rigid enclosure with integrated cooling.
3. Fitted with a module-level battery management system ("BMS") capable of cell balancing, state-of-charge estimation, and over-temperature protection.
4. Sold as a subassembly to vehicle original equipment manufacturers ("OEMs") or to battery pack integrators.

Individual battery cells, complete battery packs, and modules without integrated cooling are outside the scope of this bulletin.

## 3. Classification

Effective June 1, 2026, goods within scope shall be classified under heading **8507.60.20** (Lithium-ion accumulators, finished modules, with integrated management system). Goods entered under heading 8507.60.00 on or after that date will be subject to reclassification and post-entry amendment.

## 5. Documentation Requirements

Entries of goods within scope shall be accompanied by:

- A manufacturer's declaration identifying cell chemistry (NMC, LFP, NCA, LMO, etc.), cell form factor, nominal capacity in Wh, and module mass in kg.
- A certificate of origin issued by the competent authority of the country of manufacture, where preferential duty treatment is claimed.
- For modules originating in Country X, a Section 732(a) declaration confirming whether the merchandise is subject to, or excluded from, the additional remedy duty.

## 6. Enforcement

Post-entry audits will be conducted on a targeted basis during the twelve months following the effective date. Importers who have relied in good faith on the prior ruling issued under CB-2022-07 may request prospective-only application of the updated classification by filing Form FCTA-318 within 30 days of the effective date.
