# Guidance Notice GN-2025-04: Liquidity Coverage Reporting for Mid-Sized Banking Organizations (Synthetic)

> Synthetic regulatory notice authored for AOE testing. The issuing authority, effective date, and reporting thresholds are fictional.

**Issuing authority:** Inter-Agency Banking Supervision Council ("IBSC")
**Notice number:** GN-2025-04
**Effective date:** July 1, 2025
**Applicable to:** Banking organizations with consolidated assets between $50 billion and $250 billion ("Category III institutions")

## 1. Purpose

This notice sets forth the IBSC's expectations for the form and frequency of liquidity coverage ratio ("LCR") reporting applicable to Category III institutions. It supersedes Guidance Notice GN-2022-11 and harmonizes reporting conventions with the revised Basel III post-finalization framework.

## 2. Definitions

- **High-Quality Liquid Assets ("HQLA")** comprise Level 1 assets (central bank reserves, certain sovereign debt), Level 2A assets (qualifying corporate debt, certain covered bonds), and Level 2B assets (qualifying equities, residential mortgage-backed securities meeting the risk-weight criteria).
- **Net Cash Outflows** means expected cash outflows less expected cash inflows over a 30-calendar-day stress scenario, calculated according to the run-off rates specified in Appendix A.
- **Liquidity Coverage Ratio** means the ratio of HQLA to Net Cash Outflows, expressed as a percentage.

## 3. Reporting Requirements

1. **Form IBSC-LCR-5** shall be filed monthly, no later than the 20th calendar day following the end of each reporting month.
2. Institutions shall classify each HQLA holding into Level 1, Level 2A, or Level 2B, and shall provide the market value and the post-haircut value of each position.
3. Net cash outflows shall be decomposed by counterparty type (retail deposits, non-financial corporate deposits, financial sector deposits, secured funding, and contingent funding obligations).
4. Institutions shall attest that the reported LCR is not less than **100%** as of each month-end.

## 4. Governance Expectations

The board of directors of each Category III institution shall:

- Review the institution's liquidity risk tolerance statement at least annually.
- Receive monthly reports on LCR, net stable funding ratio ("NSFR"), and key liquidity risk indicators.
- Approve the contingency funding plan and review it following any material change in the institution's funding profile.

## 5. Enforcement

Failure to file Form IBSC-LCR-5 within the prescribed period, or filing of materially incorrect reports, may result in civil money penalties under Section 8(i) of the Inter-Agency Banking Supervision Act. Institutions whose LCR falls below 100% for three consecutive months shall submit a remediation plan to their primary supervisor within 30 days.
